# The Plan

"I am afraid, @{Watson}, that I shall have to trouble you," said @{Holmes},
settling back at @{Baker Street} with the air of a man wholly pleased with
himself. "I want you to be neutral, to do nothing, and to look on. There will
probably be some small unpleasantness. Do not join in it."

"I am to be neutral?"

"To do nothing whatever. When I raise my hand — so — you will throw into the
window what I give you to throw, and will, at the same time, raise the cry of
fire." He handed me a long, cigar-shaped roll. "It is an ordinary
@{plumber's smoke-rocket}, fitted with a cap at either end to make it
self-lighting."

"And what will follow?"

"When @{Irene Adler} is roused by the alarm, she will rush, as any woman would,
to the one thing she values most. A married woman grabs at her baby; an unmarried
one reaches for her jewel-box. There is no object dearer to our lady than
@{The Photograph}. She will fly to secure it. The alarm of fire is admirably done.
The smoke and shouting are enough to shake nerves of steel." %% the whole trick
in one paragraph — Holmes reasoning about panic, and being right about everyone
but her %%

{==You will remember me?==}{>>foreshadow — she remembers him rather better<<} he
added, half to himself, as we set out into the dusk toward @{Briony Lodge}.
