# Good-night, Mister Sherlock Holmes

We had reached @{Baker Street} and stopped at the door. @{Holmes} was searching
his pockets for the key when someone passing said:

"Good-night, Mister @{Sherlock Holmes}."

There were several people on the pavement at the time, but the greeting appeared
to come from a slim youth in an ulster who had hurried by. @{Holmes} stared down
the dimly lit street.

"I've heard that voice before," said he. "Now, I wonder who the deuce that could
have been." %% he does not yet know he has just been beaten — this line is the
hinge of the whole story %%

I slept at @{Baker Street} that night, and we were engaged upon our toast and
coffee in the morning when @{the King of Bohemia} rushed into the room.

"You have really got it!" he cried, seizing @{Sherlock Holmes} by either
shoulder and looking eagerly into his face.

"Not yet."

"But you have hopes?"

"I have hopes."

"Then come. I am all impatience to be gone." And the three of us set off together
for @{Briony Lodge}.
