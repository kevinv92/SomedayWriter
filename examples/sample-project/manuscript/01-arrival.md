# Arrival

Rain came sideways off the harbor the night Mara first saw the ship. It flew no
flag she recognized, and that alone was enough to keep her in the shadow of the
crane-house, counting the figures that came down the gangway.

Corvin was already there, of course. The captain always seemed to know before
anyone else which tides would carry trouble. %% does Corvin recognize the ship
here, or later? check ch.2 %% He did not look at Mara, but she knew he had seen
her.

"You shouldn't be here," Corvin said, to no one, to the rain.

Mara stayed exactly where she was.
