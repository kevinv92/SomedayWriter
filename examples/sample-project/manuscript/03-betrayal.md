# Betrayal

The message Mara carried was a name, and the name was Corvin's.

She stood a long time in the dark of the crane-house — the same shadow where this
had started — turning the sealed paper over in her scarred hands. To deliver it
was to end him. To burn it was to end herself, and everyone the rebellion had
sworn to protect.

When Corvin came, he came alone, the way he always did.

"You already know," he said.

"I know," said Mara. And for the first time, she let him see her face.
