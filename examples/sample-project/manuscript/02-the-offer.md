# The Offer

By morning the ship had a name and the name had a price. Mara heard it twice
before noon — once as a rumor, once as a threat.

Corvin found her at the fish market. "They're recruiting," he said. "Quietly.
The kind of quiet that gets people killed."

"And you're telling me because?"

"Because you're the only courier in Harborwick nobody's bought yet." He almost
smiled. "The rebellion needs someone who can carry a message without reading it."

Mara read every message. Corvin knew that too.
